Candlestick Patterns

Doji

A Doji is a candlestick pattern where the open and close prices are nearly equal. It signals market indecision and potential reversal.

Hammer

The Hammer is a candlestick pattern with a small body at the top and a long lower shadow. It suggests a potential reversal.

Bullish Hammer: The Bullish Hammer occurs at the bottom of a downtrend and signals a potential bullish reversal.

Bearish Hammer: The Bearish Hammer occurs at the top of an uptrend and signals a potential bearish reversal.

Engulfing

The Engulfing pattern occurs when a larger candlestick completely engulfs the previous one. It signals a potential trend reversal.

Bullish Engulfing: A bullish reversal pattern where a small bearish candle is followed by a larger bullish candle.

Bearish Engulfing: A bearish reversal pattern where a small bullish candle is followed by a larger bearish candle.
